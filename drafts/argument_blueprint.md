## Argument Blueprint

### Central Thesis

This paper argues that **Zevnik's "void," Bonta/Protevi's "plane of immanence," and Žižek's "the Real" constitute three distinct layers of a unified ontological analysis — rather than three competing concepts occupying the same theoretical space — because** (a) each concept operates at a different level of abstraction (pre-ontological ground vs. structured generative condition vs. anti-ontological remainder), (b) the relationships between them are structurally hierarchical (condition → mechanism → surplus-effect) rather than mutually exclusive, and (c) their integration provides an analytical framework capable of penetrating both institutional (international law) and biological (pandemic biopolitics) domains of political reality with consistent explanatory force.

---

### Sub-Arguments

#### Sub-Argument 1: The void is an affirmative "nothing," not a nothingness of lack — it is the pre-ontological condition of all order, not an absence that demands filling.

**Claim**: Zevnik's void concept provides a non-essentialist ontological foundation for political analysis that avoids both nihilism and the "lack" structure of early Lacanian thought.

**Evidence**:
- Zevnik (2016): "Nothing exists in the materiality that inherently directs forms of knowledge"—the third sense of "nothing" is affirmative and pre-ontological [E10]
- Zevnik's distinction from Prozorov's "void universalism": the void is "not-all" rather than "all," meaning it cannot be fully knowledge-ized [E10 vs. E7]
- Özselçuk & Madra (2021): the void as condition of possibility for formalization, not as emptiness [N14]

**Reasoning**: If the void were just "nothing" in the nihilist sense, it would be politically paralyzing (nothing matters). If it were "lack," it would presuppose a positive term that is missing. Zevnik's third sense — affirmative nothing — is the only one that supports the claim that "all orders are contingent but new orders can still emerge." This is the foundational claim of the entire three-layer framework.

**Counter-argument**: Molloy (2017) questions whether the void can sustain political agency — if everything is equally contingent, what grounds political choice? [N3]

**Rebuttal strategy**: Concede and limit. The void does not by itself ground choice — that's precisely why we need the second layer (plane of immanence). The void is the condition of possibility for agency, not its sufficient ground.

**Argument strength**: Strong (multiple converging evidence streams, clear counter-argument with built-in rebuttal via layering strategy)

---

#### Sub-Argument 2: The plane of immanence is not a synonym for the void, but a distinct layer — it provides the structured topological space of virtual attractors that fills the "how" gap left by the void's "that it can be otherwise."

**Claim**: Distinguishing the plane of immanence (structured virtual tendency) from the void (unstructured affirmative nothing) resolves a fundamental tension in Zevnik's own work and provides the operative mechanism for the three-layer framework.

**Evidence**:
- Bonta & Protevi (2004): attractors as "structures for the self-organization of processes" — the plane of immanence is a topological space of virtual tendencies, not an empty container [E3]
- DeLanda (2006): Deleuzian social ontology as assemblage theory — the plane of immanence is the condition of possibility for emergent wholes, not a undifferentiated ground [N10]
- Müller (2015): assemblages as socio-material power configurations — the plane of immanence is always already structured by material conditions [S2]
- Protevi (2010): political affect as the embodied dimension of the plane of immanence — connecting abstract ontology to concrete political bodies [N5]

**Reasoning**: If the plane of immanence is collapsed into the void (as Zevnik arguably does), the framework can say "orders are contingent" but cannot explain how particular orders emerge rather than others. The plane of immanence, operationalized through virtual attractors (Bonta/Protevi) or assemblages (DeLanda/Müller), provides the missing mechanism: different material configurations produce different attractor landscapes, which constrain (without determining) the possible paths of actualization.

**Counter-argument**: If the plane of immanence has structure (attractors, tendencies), is it really a form of "immanence" in the Deleuzian sense, or does it reintroduce a hidden transcendence?

**Rebuttal strategy**: Reframe. The attractors are immanent in the sense that they are emergent properties of the system itself, not imposed from outside. They are real but not transcendent — they exist as virtual tendencies of the material system, not as Platonic forms. This is the core innovation of Bonta/Protevi's connection of Deleuze to complexity science.

**Argument strength**: Strong (clear distinction from Sub-Argument 1, robust evidence chain, convincing reframing of the transcendence objection)

---

#### Sub-Argument 3: The Real is not a third ontological layer but an anti-ontological surplus-effect — every symbolization operation produces a remainder that cannot be symbolized, and this remainder is the Real.

**Claim**: The Real operates at a different logical level than the void (ontological) and the plane of immanence (ontological): it is the "shadow" of ontological operations, not an independent ontological domain.

**Evidence**:
- 赵淳 (2024): "surplus" as cross-domain theoretical model connecting surplus value, surplus jouissance, and ideological surplus — any system's operation produces excess beyond self-maintenance [E12]
- Kinnvall & Svensson (2023): COVID-19 deepened abjectification — the Real as the return of what the symbolic order excluded (racialized bodies, migrant labor) [E4]
- Heron (2022): toy with the law — perversion as revealing the Real of legal order, the contingency beneath the symbolic [N2]

**Reasoning**: The void and the plane of immanence answer "how is existence possible?" The Real answers "why is every ontological account incomplete?" The two types of question are logically different. This difference is captured by 赵淳's "surplus" model: surplus (the Real) is an effect of system operation (symbolization), not a pre-existing condition. The void is the condition of surplus (affirmative nothing → possibility of excess), but it is not itself surplus.

**Counter-argument**: Lacanians might argue that reducing the Real to a "surplus-effect" loses the traumatic kernel of the Real — its irreducible, unassimilable character is not just "what's left over" but a positive antagonism.

**Rebuttal strategy**: Acknowledge and limit. The "surplus" framing does not deny the Real's traumatic character; it merely specifies its structural position. The Real is traumatic precisely because it is a remainder that resists integration — this is what "surplus" means in the psychoanalytic register. The framework does not dissolve the Real into something harmless; it locates it precisely as that which cannot be located within ontological positivity.

**Argument strength**: Strong (the key distinction of the entire framework, supported by multiple converging evidence streams, credible counter-argument handled)

---

#### Sub-Argument 4: The integrated three-layer framework produces analytically distinguishable insights at each layer when applied to concrete political phenomena, as demonstrated by the international law and COVID-19 cases.

**Claim**: The framework's analytical value is not merely theoretical — it generates distinct, non-redundant findings at each layer when applied to empirical cases.

**Evidence**:
- Roberts (2018): divisible college — same facts produce opposite legal conclusions; this is visible at all three levels (void: no essential legal order; immanence: divergent attractor basins; Real: return of great-power politics) [E8]
- Messingschlager (2026): selective legalism shifts the attractor structure from "verdict economy" to "process outcomes" — an immanence-level intervention with Real-level consequences [E6]
- Kinnvall & Svensson (2023): COVID-19 exposes the void (contingency of health order), operates within pre-existing stratification (immanence), and produces abjectification as Real-effect

**Reasoning**: A single-layer analysis (e.g., only the void → everything is contingent) would be too abstract to distinguish between different forms of contingency. A two-layer analysis (void + immanence) would miss the remainder that every order produces. Only three layers together can simultaneously capture: (a) that the order could be otherwise (void), (b) why it is this way rather than another (immanence), and (c) what it represses/rejects (Real). The empirical cases demonstrate that each layer generates a different type of insight, and none is reducible to the others.

**Counter-argument**: The framework may be overdetermined — does each case truly need all three layers, or could a simpler framework produce the same insights?

**Rebuttal strategy**: Reframe. The point is not that every analysis must use all three layers, but that a framework capable of shifting between layers can capture phenomena that single-layer frameworks miss. The "selective legalism" case, for instance, only makes full sense when read across layers: the void analysis captures the contingency, the immanence analysis captures the attractor-shift strategy, and the Real analysis captures the ineradicable cost of exclusion.

**Argument strength**: Moderate-Strong (empirical evidence is solid, but the "necessity of three layers" claim depends on the reader accepting the non-redundancy argument — this is the most contestable claim)

---

#### Sub-Argument 5: The framework must confront its own self-reflexive limit — Bonta's "cryptic rhizomatics" challenge calls into question the emancipatory assumptions of the Deleuzian tradition that the framework relies on.

**Claim**: The framework's greatest vulnerability is that its own conceptual tools are not immune to the power structures it analyzes — rhizomatic organization can serve domination as easily as liberation.

**Evidence**:
- Bonta (2009): "The State and the transnational corporation long ago learned every trick of the autonomous rhizome" — cryptic rhizomatics as the dark double of Deleuzian politics [E2]
- MacFarlane (2017): management studies have directly appropriated Deleuzian concepts as surplus-extraction technologies [E5]
- Vergne (2015): the missing organizational level in Deleuzian analysis — orgology as the necessary supplement [E9]
- Buchanan (2015, 2017): assemblage theory's limitations — the concept has been stretched to the point of vacuity [S3][S4]
- Read (2008): the micro-macro link problem — how do micro-political assemblages produce macro-entities? [S7]

**Reasoning**: If the framework aims to be a tool for critical political analysis but its own concepts (rhizome, plane of immanence, line of flight) can be — and have been — turned into instruments of domination, then the framework's critical purchase is not automatic. It must build in self-reflexivity as a structural feature, not an afterthought.

**Counter-argument**: This critique is self-defeating — if all concepts can be appropriated, then the critique itself can be appropriated, making any critical position impossible.

**Rebuttal strategy**: Acknowledge and limit. The self-reflexive critique is not paralyzing; it forces the framework to avoid the "emancipatory guarantee" fallacy. The response (§6.3) is not to abandon the framework but to: (1) recognize that the plane of immanence has both liberatory and predatory modalities, (2) ensure the framework remains open to its own critique, (3) treat the conceptual apparatus as a working tool, not a closed system.

**Argument strength**: Strong (well-supported by evidence, the self-defeating objection is convincingly handled — this is actually the framework's most sophisticated move)

### Supplementary Argument: Taiji (太极) as a cross-cultural conceptual resonance

The three-layer framework can be illuminated through a resonance with the Taiji (太极/太極) cosmology in Chinese philosophical tradition. This is presented not as a claim about philosophical lineage (the framework derives from European sources) but as a **conceptual analogy** that aids communication and reveals deeper structural affinities.

**Analogy**:

- **The Void (虚空) ≅ Taiji (太极)** — The undifferentiated whole that encompasses both poles; the "supreme ultimate" that is not itself a thing but the condition of all differentiation. In Zhou Dunyi's *Taijitu Shuo* (太极图说), Taiji is "the non-ultimate and also the supreme ultimate" (无极而太极) — a formulation strikingly close to Zevnik's "affirmative nothingness."
- **The Plane of Immanence ≅ Yang (阳)** — The structured generative tendency; the pole of differentiation, creativity, and actualization. Yang is not separate from the Taiji but is its manifest aspect.
- **The Real ≅ Yin (阴)** — The pole of remainder, resistance, and what escapes symbolization. Yin is the latent, the shadow, the excess that every yang-actualization leaves behind.
- **The interpenetrating dots** (阴中有阳, 阳中有阴) map onto: within every structured order (immanence) there is a remainder that resists (Real); within every remainder there is the seed of a new order (immanence).

This analogy serves multiple functions:

1. **Pedagogical**: Provides an intuitive visual-conceptual anchor for readers familiar (or unfamiliar) with the framework
2. **Cross-cultural**: Locates the framework within a global intellectual landscape, countering the Eurocentrism critique that continental philosophy dominates IR ontology debates
3. **Structural deepening**: The yin-yang mutual containment precisely captures the relationship between immanence and the Real — they are not separate "layers" in a hierarchy but mutually defining poles within the encompassing void
4. **Self-reflexive payoff**: The Taiji image itself can be co-opted (the CCP's "harmonious society" discourse uses taiji-like rhetoric), connecting back to the §6 critique of concept appropriation

**Placement**: §4 (three-layer model), as a coda to the formal framework presentation — a "thinking with" supplement rather than a core argument. Extended briefly in the Conclusion.

**Theoretical caution**: This is a **resonance, not a derivation**. The paper should not claim that Deleuze or Lacan were "really" doing Chinese philosophy. The Taiji analogy is offered as a heuristic and a bridge — a way of "doing philosophy as concept creation" (德勒兹) by bringing different traditions into encounter.

1. **Tone**: Assertive when distinguishing void from plane of immanence (SA2 is the paper's most original theoretical contribution); appropriately cautious when claiming three-layer necessity (SA4 is the most vulnerable claim — use "demonstrates" rather than "proves").
2. **Hedging language**: On SA4, use phrases like "the cases suggest that three-layer analysis captures dimensions that single-layer approaches would miss" rather than "three layers are necessary."
3. **Emphasis points**:
   - The void/plane distinction (2.3) is the paper's most important single move — treat it as such
   - The "void is the ontological condition of surplus, surplus is the subjective effect of void" formula (3.3) should be highlighted as a key conceptual contribution
   - The self-reflexive turn (§6) should not read as defensive but as confident — the framework is strong enough to critique itself
4. **Key transitions to strengthen**:
   - §1 → §2: "void says 'it could be otherwise' but not 'how otherwise'" — make this transition explicit and memorable
   - §3 → §4: "analyzing the shadow (Real) reveals the limits of the ontology; integrating all three produces the full picture"
5. **Citation density**: SA3 (Real/surplus section) needs the most citations per paragraph as it makes the most heterodox claim (Real as surplus-effect); SA2 (plane of immanence) needs fewer as it stays closer to established Deleuzian scholarship.
