### Annotated Bibliography

#### Existing Sources (12)

**[E1]** Atal, M.R. & Veldman, J. (2025). *The concurrent emergence of conflicting social ontologies of the corporation*. Cambridge UP.
- **Type**: Book
- **Key Findings**: Corporations strategically shift between different legal ontology definitions (person, asset bundle, contract nexus), creating a "fractal drift" in corporate ontology.
- **Relevance**: Directly supports §6.2 blind spot #3 — corporate ontological drift complicates the void/immanence distinction.
- **Quality**: High (university press monograph)
- **Potential Use**: §6 (Critical self-reflection / Blind spots)

**[E2]** Bonta, M. (2009). The Multitude and its Doppelganger. *ACME*, 8(2): 245-277.
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key Findings**: Introduces "cryptic rhizomatics" — global corporatocracy has learned all the tricks of the autonomous rhizome.
- **Relevance**: Core critical resource for §6.1 — the most incisive self-critique of the Deleuzian framework
- **Quality**: High (peer-reviewed, well-cited)
- **Potential Use**: §6.1 (核心批评来源)

**[E3]** Bonta, M. & Protevi, J. (2004). *Deleuze and Geophilosophy: A Guide and Glossary*. Edinburgh UP.
- **Type**: Book (monograph/glossary)
- **Key Findings**: Connects Deleuze-Guattari to complexity science; develops plane of immanence as virtual attractor topology; distinguishes stratified vs consistent system modalities.
- **Relevance**: Core source for §2 (Plane of Immanence). The attractor-as-structure concept is the operationalization bridge.
- **Quality**: High (university press, foundational in the field)
- **Potential Use**: §2 (whole section), §4 (three-layer model)

**[E4]** Kinnvall, C. & Svensson, T. (2023). A Postcolonial Reworking of Lacan's Mirror Image and the Real. *International Studies Quarterly*, 67(3).
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key Findings**: COVID-19 deepened abjectification of marginalized bodies; uses Lacanian Real to analyze pandemic's unequal impact through postcolonial lens.
- **Relevance**: Primary empirical case for §5.3 (COVID-19 as return of the Real)
- **Quality**: High (top IR journal)
- **Potential Use**: §5.3, §5.4

**[E5]** MacFarlane, K. (2017). Management studies and Deleuzian appropriation. *Progress in Human Geography*.
- **Type**: Journal article (review essay)
- **Key Findings**: Management studies have directly appropriated Deleuzian concepts (rhizome, smooth space, plane of immanence) as tools for optimizing surplus extraction.
- **Relevance**: Core critical resource for §6.2 blind spot #5 — concepts themselves not immune to capital capture
- **Quality**: High (top geography journal)
- **Potential Use**: §6.2

**[E6]** Messingschlager (2026). Selective Legalism and China's Ordercraft. *Yale Journal of International Affairs*.
- **Type**: Journal article
- **Key Findings**: China selectively participates in treaty frameworks while resisting binding adjudication, shifting disputes from "verdict economy" to "process outcomes."
- **Relevance**: Core empirical case for §5.2 (Selective Legalism)
- **Quality**: High (peer-reviewed policy journal)
- **Potential Use**: §5.2

**[E7]** Prozorov, S. (2014). *Ontology and World Politics: Void Universalism I*. Routledge.
- **Type**: Book (monograph)
- **Key Findings**: Develops "void universalism" — the void as the condition for all (all-inclusive universality).
- **Relevance**: Critical dialogue partner for §1.3; Zevnik explicitly distances herself from Prozorov's "all" as opposed to "not-all."
- **Quality**: High (major IR theory monograph)
- **Potential Use**: §1.3 (Void section), §4 (model differentiation)

**[E8]** Roberts, A. (2018). Divisible College vs Invisible College. In *OUP Handbook of International Law*.
- **Type**: Book chapter
- **Key Findings**: International law scholars operate in parallel "silos" producing divergent legal interpretations of the same facts (Crimea, South China Sea).
- **Relevance**: Core empirical case for §5.1 (Divisible College)
- **Quality**: High (OUP Handbook)
- **Potential Use**: §5.1

**[E9]** Vergne, J.-P. (2015). An Orgology of Capitalism. Ivey Business School.
- **Type**: Working paper / report
- **Key Findings**: Deleuze's three syntheses are only applied at individual-desire and state levels, missing the intermediate organizational level.
- **Relevance**: Core critical resource for §6.2 blind spot #1 — organizational level gap
- **Potential Use**: §6.2

**[E10]** Zevnik, A. (2016). *Lacan, Deleuze and World Politics*. Routledge.
- **Type**: Book (monograph)
- **Key Findings**: "Void" as affirmative nothingness — the ultimate ontological support of the world is an affirmative nothing, an ocean of possibilities; distinguishes three senses of "nothing" (nihilist, lack, affirmative); distances from Prozorov's "all."
- **Relevance**: **Core source for the entire framework** — §1 (Void) and §4 (three-layer model)
- **Quality**: High (major IR-Continental Philosophy intervention)
- **Potential Use**: §1 (entire section), §3, §4

**[E11]** Zevnik, A. (2016). Chapter 5: Lacan, Deleuze and the Politics of the Face. In Nedoh & Zevnik (eds.), *Lacan and Deleuze*. Edinburgh UP. DOI: 10.1515/9781474408301-007
- **Type**: Book chapter
- **Key Findings**: Extends the void framework to the politics of the face/visage — the face as a site where the void is both covered and exposed.
- **Relevance**: Supplementary to [E10]; shows application of void framework to a specific political site
- **Quality**: High (edited volume by same scholar)
- **Potential Use**: §1.4 (Void's political implications)

**[E12]** 赵淳 (2024). '剩余'模型：从马克思主义到精神分析学. *外国文学*, 2024(4).
- **Type**: Journal article (Chinese)

- **Relevance**: Core source for §3.2 (surplus as cross-domain model) and §3.4 (surplus jouissance analysis including the屠夫妻子和阿Q examples)

### Identified Gaps and Supplementary Finds

#### Gap 1: Chinese-language scholarship on Deleuze/Lacan in IR/social sciences

**Supplementary search conducted** (CrossRef + OpenAlex, plus targeted author queries).

**Finding**: Chinese-language journal databases (CNKI, Airiti, CSSCI, 万方) are not indexed by CrossRef or OpenAlex. The APIs used in this search cannot retrieve Chinese-language articles. To properly fill this gap, a dedicated CNKI/Airiti search is needed.

**However, the following relevant Chinese-language or China-related scholarship was identified indirectly**:

- 赵淳 (2024) — already included [E12], foundational for the surplus-as-cross-domain-model argument
- 王相辉 (2023) Deleuze Image Ontology / Movement-Image — in *Art Research Letters* (low quality, peripheral)
- Several Chinese scholars publish on Deleuze/Lacan in English-language venues (see Gap 2 findings below)

**Recommendation**: For a 12,000-word Chinese-language paper targeting a Chinese journal, a dedicated CNKI search for the following keywords would be valuable: 德勒兹/国际关系/本体论, 拉康/政治哲学/剩余快感, 内在性平面, 虚空/政治, 齐泽克/意识形态. This is beyond the current API search capability but can be conducted manually by the author.

#### Gap 3: Gender/postcolonial dimension
Kinnvall & Svensson (2023) brings postcolonial analysis, but the draft doesn't engage with the feminist or gender dimensions of Lacanian/Deleuzian ontology. Sources on sexual difference, the body, and biopolitics would add depth.

#### Gap 4: Empirical testing beyond international law and COVID
The framework is tested on international law and COVID-19. Additional test domains — e.g., AI governance, climate change politics, border/migration politics — would strengthen the framework's generalizability claim. [S6] (sportswashing/assemblage) provides one additional avenue.
