# The limit of thought and the logic of law: Zevnik uses Foucault (sovereign discourse) to bridge Lacan's symbolic order and Deleuze's ordering principles — making visible how sovereign law co-opts resistance

## 原文要点

> Foucault in Order of Things: "the representation of the world today is a product of particular sovereign discourses and representational practices that shape forms of being [...] The key political question is: how is one to alter forms of representation, or how can one do away with, play or take advantage of the existing representational patterns?"

> Lacan on resistance (the 1968 protests): "resistance is bound to institute 'just another' sovereign." This is because "the conditions which predetermine the existence of the political space and the political subject (most prominently language and law) will inevitably 'return' acts of resistance to their starting place."

> Zevnik's countermove: "following a discourse of the hysteric [...] the statement suggests that the engagement with resistance needs to derive from a different thought." The "limit of thought" — pressing the ontological boundaries of the symbolic order — reveals that the existing ordering is contingent and other logics are possible.

> The Guantánamo habeas litigation: even successful legal challenges did not fundamentally alter the sovereign discourse that defines detainees as outside law — the legal order "incorporated the challenge without changing its fundamental logic."

## 我的判断

这一章节群（第2章+第5章）处理了全书最棘手的理论难题：如果主权话语/符号秩序/法律构型始终会把抵抗"带回"到其起点（如拉康评论1968年运动所言），那么任何形式的政治抵抗如何能够产生真正的变化？Zevnik 的答案不是"寻找一个未被染指的外部空间"——她知道不存在这种空间——而是打破"思想自身设下的限制"（the limit of thought）。她使用福柯在《词与物》中对古典表征话语的谱系学来展示：主权的"秩序逻辑"（ordering logic）不是自然的，而是历史地产生的——因此也可以重新排列。关塔那摩的 habeas corpus 诉讼案例就是最好的佐证：被拘留者通过法律渠道争取释放，但即使胜诉，也没有改变主权逻辑本身——法律"吸收了挑战但没有改变其基本逻辑"。那么出路在哪里？Zevnik 说并不在于赢得法律斗争，而在于"思想以外"——即通过对符号秩序的边界进行持续不断的考验，揭示其背后的"空"（void/ nothingness），从而打开其他构型的可能性。这就把她带回了一元论本体论的核心：因为存在的最终支撑是空，所以任何秩序逻辑都只是诸多可能性中的一种。抵抗的伦理任务不是推翻主权者，而是通过持续地把自己推向符号秩序的边缘来揭示其偶然性。
