# From bare life to bodily-becomings: Zevnik's Deleuzian reading of Guantánamo detainee resistance as a politics of "becoming-detainee" and "becoming-animal"

## 原文要点

> Zevnik traces how the Guantánamo detainee is produced as bare life (Agamben) through the sovereign decision that excludes them from legal protection. But she argues that "the image of a detainee as bare life still troubled me — it has emerged that not only the detainees but also the guards, the lawyers and the military personnel, all in different ways part of the Guantánamo puzzle, similarly suffered."

> Drawing on Deleuze's becoming-animal (the wolf pack, the sorcerer's pact), Zevnik conceptualizes "becoming-detainee": "It is not that the detainee becomes an animal but that through the process of detention and its various practices, the detainee enters a space of non-humanity, a space where the human/animal distinction collapses. This space is not one of degradation but of a different form of existence."

> Body practices — hunger strikes, refusal to wear prison clothes, prayer, noise-making — are not merely acts of resistance within the existing order. They are "expressions of a self that does not correspond to the sovereign/legal language," a "politics of appearance" that "penetrates a grander political narrative."

## 我的判断

Zevnik 对关塔那摩被拘留者的分析是她整部书最具原创性的应用部分，也是对阿甘本"赤裸生命"（bare life）框架的一次重要批判和超越。她并不是否认阿甘本的分析框架在描述层面是准确的——被拘留者确实被主权例外排除在法律保护之外，退化为"神圣人"。但她的问题是：**然后呢？** 如果被拘留者仅仅是赤裸生命，那么他们就完全被还原为纯粹的被剥夺状态——没有任何政治能动性。Zevnik 通过德勒兹的"生成-动物"（becoming-animal）——不是变成动物的模拟，而是进入一个人类/动物区分崩溃的空间——来发现被拘留者如何通过身体实践创建了一种主权和法律语言无法捕捉的政治存在形式。绝食抗议——不仅要求权利，而且通过拒绝进食来拒绝监狱为身体定义的"正常"模式——是一种"出现于世界"（appearance in the world）的表达。Zevnik 将德勒兹的"成为-少数"（becoming-minoritarian）概念与拉康的"非全"（not-all）连接起来：被拘留者处于"非全"的空间——既不完全被法律界定，也不完全被排斥在法律之外——这恰恰使他们的身体成为政治表达的媒介。这一论证对"剩余安全"有直接启发：安全例外机制产生的不只是赤裸生命，还有一种法律无法完全驯化的"剩余存在形式"。
